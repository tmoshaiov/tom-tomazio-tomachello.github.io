Exercise qln. Let $G$ be a finite commutative group. Then
i. If $g_1,\dots,g_k\in G$ have pairwise coprime orders, then $\ord(g_1\dots g_k)=\ord(g_1)\dots\ord(g_k)$. Moreover $\gen{g_1,\dots,g_k}=\gen{g_1\dots g_k}$
ii. The set $\set{\ord(g):g\in G}$ is closed under $\lcm$.
iii. $G$ is cyclic if it has order $n$ and $\forall p\mid n \exists g\in G : g^{n/p}\neq \id$.

Exercise qgk. Let $G$ be a group, $\F$ be a field.
i. Any collection of distinct homomorphisms $\phi_1,\dots,\phi_k : G\to \F^{\times}$ is lineaerly independent. Namely $a_1\phi_1+\dots+a_k\phi_k\equiv 0\implies a_1=\dots=a_k=0$.
ii. If $G$ is finite, then the number of homomorphisms $G\to \F^\times$ is at most the number of elements of $G$.


Exercise uhh. Let $G$ be a group with $n=[G:Z(G)]$ finite. Then
i. $[x,y]^{n+1} = [x,y^2] [yxy\inv]^{n-1}$ holds $\forall x,y\in G$.
ii. $G$ has at most $n^2$ commutators.
iii. $G'$ is finite (in fact, $\abs{G'}\le n^{2n^3}$).
